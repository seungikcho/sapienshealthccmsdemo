# Sapiens Health — Digital Health homepage direction

Sapiens Health should now present itself as a **Digital Health AI company** built for clinical workflows rather than as a cross-vertical multimodal platform. The homepage should align with the Nebius Digital Health framing by emphasizing that the company transforms **clinical workflows** through **real-world data intelligence** and works under the practical constraint of **incomplete multimodal clinical data**.

The new homepage narrative should move away from split verticals such as Clinical Intelligence versus Bioprocess Intelligence. Instead, it should tell one coherent story: hospitals rarely have complete imaging, EHR, notes, vitals, or pathology at the same moment, but MedMIX can still convert fragmented clinical reality into usable predictions and then into workflow-ready insights.

The hero should frame the company around incomplete multimodal clinical data and digital health outcomes. Supporting sections should show how prediction tasks become actionable insights for clinical teams. Instead of broad “intelligence” language, the visual and copy structure should emphasize examples such as deterioration risk, discharge readiness, readmission risk, treatment response, or escalation alerts becoming workflow guidance, prioritization signals, and operational insight.

The framework section should describe a simple path: messy real-world clinical inputs enter, missing data is masked rather than fabricated, MedMIX fuses available signals, and the system returns clinical insights that support patient interaction, prioritization, and workflow action. All manufacturing and CDMO language should be removed from this version.
