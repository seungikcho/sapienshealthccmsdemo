# Reference notes for homepage redesign

## Beacon Health

Beacon Health uses a very direct startup landing-page structure. The hero states the category immediately, follows with one bold sentence, and pairs it with two clear CTAs. The visual system emphasizes a product-like workflow demonstration rather than decorative imagery. Below the hero, the site quickly moves into trust signals, a structured “How it Works” explanation, integration coverage, concrete example workflows, and a strong final conversion section.

The most reusable cues are the restrained navigation, the minimal number of top-level claims, and the way operational complexity is condensed into a few strong sections. For Sapiens Health, the useful takeaway is not the exact copy style but the disciplined prioritization of product clarity over storytelling sprawl.

## Pelica Health

Pelica Health presents a more editorial and refined composition. The hero is spacious, the typography carries much of the visual identity, and the UI card on the right acts as a clean product proof rather than a busy illustration. The page also uses modular category cards and section transitions that feel premium while keeping copy compact.

The strongest reusable cues are the clear visual hierarchy, disciplined whitespace, and the way one product thesis is repeated in slightly different forms across the page. For Sapiens Health, this suggests a direction with fewer but stronger sections, premium typography, and a card-based product explanation that feels credible and calm.

## Design takeaways for Sapiens Health

Sapiens Health should combine Beacon’s clarity with Pelica’s restraint. The landing page should avoid old MedMIX messaging entirely and instead center the user-provided thesis: fragmented patient data creates an execution bottleneck, and Sapiens provides an AI-native execution layer through Connect, Prioritize, and Execute.

A strong structure would be a concise hero, a problem framing section, a three-card execution flow, a proof-style section explaining what changes for care teams, and a compact final CTA. Visually, the page should use clean rounded cards, calm spacing, limited accent color, and custom line-based icons or lightweight product graphics rather than overly illustrative scenes.
